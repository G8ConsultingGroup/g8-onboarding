INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR ENGAGEMENT AGREEMENT

G8 Consulting Group · Network Partner Agreement · Tax Year 2026

This Independent Contractor Engagement Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the date of electronic execution between G8 Consulting Group, a New York entity, with its principal place of business at 157 Columbus Avenue, New York, New York 10023 (the "Company"), and the undersigned party ("Contractor"), collectively the "Parties."

1. Engagement

The Company engages Contractor to perform the services described in the onboarding intake form (the "Services"), which is incorporated herein by reference. The Company may update the scope of Services from time to time by written notice, including email.

2. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. Contractor has full control over the manner and means by which the Services are performed. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship. Contractor is solely responsible for all taxes, withholdings, insurance, and benefits associated with compensation received under this Agreement.

3. Compensation & Payment

The Company shall pay Contractor the rate and on the schedule specified in the onboarding intake form. Payment is contingent upon Contractor's satisfactory performance of the Services. The Company reserves the right to withhold payment for Services not rendered or not meeting agreed-upon standards. Contractor shall submit invoices or time records as reasonably requested.

4. Tax Obligations & 1099 Reporting

Contractor acknowledges that the Company will not withhold federal, state, or local income taxes, Social Security, Medicare, unemployment, or any other taxes from payments made under this Agreement. Contractor is solely responsible for reporting and paying all applicable taxes on compensation received. The Company will issue IRS Form 1099-NEC for total payments of $2,000 or more in any calendar year, in accordance with IRC §6041A and applicable IRS regulations. Contractor agrees to provide accurate W-9 information and promptly notify the Company of any changes to taxpayer identification information.

5. Confidentiality

Contractor agrees to hold in strict confidence all non-public information related to the Company's operations, clients, strategies, and proprietary systems ("Confidential Information"). Contractor shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Company, and shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

6. Intellectual Property

All work product, deliverables, documents, reports, and materials created by Contractor in connection with the Services shall be the exclusive property of the Company and shall constitute works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law. Contractor assigns to the Company all rights, title, and interest in any such work product.

7. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, Contractor agrees not to directly solicit or engage any client, school, or institution introduced through the Company for the same or substantially similar services without the Company's prior written consent.

8. Term & Termination

This Agreement commences on the engagement start date and continues until the completion of Services or until terminated. Either Party may terminate this Agreement with seven (7) days written notice. The Company may terminate immediately for cause, including breach of this Agreement, failure to perform, or conduct detrimental to the Company's reputation or operations.

9. Representations

Contractor represents and warrants that: (a) Contractor has full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) performance of the Services will not violate any obligation to a third party; (c) all information provided during onboarding is accurate and complete; and (d) Contractor holds all licenses, certifications, and qualifications required to perform the Services.

10. Limitation of Liability

In no event shall the Company's liability to Contractor exceed the total payments made in the sixty (60) days prior to the event giving rise to the claim. Neither Party shall be liable for consequential, indirect, incidental, or punitive damages.

11. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York. Any dispute arising out of or related to this Agreement shall first be submitted to non-binding mediation. If mediation is unsuccessful, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in New York County, New York, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

12. Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the onboarding intake form, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter herein and supersedes all prior negotiations or representations. This Agreement may be modified only by a written amendment signed by both Parties.

This Agreement is executed electronically.
